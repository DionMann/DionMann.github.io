Research Interests
======
Broadly speaking, my interests are differential topology and mathematical physics. Currently, I am studying mathematical gauge theory. Gauge theory is the study of connections on principal bundles, which can be used to model the fundamental forces of physics. I am interested in how to generalize gauge theory to more complicated spaces, such as the path space to a differentiable manifold (called *higher gauge theory*). One approach I have been working on is applying Souriau's theory of *diffeology*, for which Iglesias-Zemmour has already introduced a notion of *connection* for. Since diffeology can study path spaces (one says, technically, that the category of diffeological spaces is cartesian closed), the diffeological framework seems the most appropriate for higher gauge theory.

Previously, at the University of Colorado, Boulder, I submitted a [senior thesis](https://dionmann.github.io/seniorThesis) in deformation quantization to obtain honors. Deformation quantization is a quantization program introduced by F. Bayen, M. Flato, C. Fronsdal, A. Lichnerowicz, D. Sternheimer and involves an algebraic deformation (according to M. Gerstenhaber in [this paper](https://www.jstor.org/stable/1970484?seq=1)) of \\(C^\infty(M)\\), the algebra of smooth functions on a Poisson manifold \\(M\\). I remain interested in questions about mathematical quantization, although my interests have evolved into other areas while being in graduate school.
