We are familiar with modeling the fabric of our universe as a “spacetime,” mathematically understanding a four-dimensional manifold (three spatial and one temporal dimension). Yet, modern physical theories appear to naturally require extra dimensions regarded as “internal.” Our main example is electromagnetic theory, which seems to require our universe having five dimensions (up from four). It turns out that these ideas have a beautiful explanation in mathematics: fiber bundles. This talk is an expository discussion of the theory of fiber bundles and its elegant formulations of physical laws. No prior background in differential geometry or physics will be assumed.
