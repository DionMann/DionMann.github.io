The theory of *diffeology* emerged arounds the 80s due to Jean-Marie Souriau, which is still in development today (mostly led by Souriau's former student, Patrick Iglesias-Zemmour). A *diffeology* on a set is a certain, abstract smooth structure which is comprised of functions which one wants to consider "smooth." We present the basic ideas of diffeology and discuss how differential geometry naturally emerges from this more general structure. No background information in geometry or topology is assumed!
